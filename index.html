The Importance of Regular Eye Exams

IA comprehensive eye exam is a cornerstone of good eye care. Even if you have perfect vision, it's crucial to have regular checkups to detect early signs of eye disease or health conditions that manifest in the eyes, such as diabetes or high blood pressure. At EYEDEAL EYECARE, our full-service eye exams check your visual acuity, peripheral vision, eye coordination, depth perception, and more.

Many people don't realize how vital these exams are until it's too late. Some eye conditions, like glaucoma or macular degeneration, progress silently and can cause irreversible damage before symptoms arise. Our eye doctors use advanced tools like retinal imaging and optical coherence tomography to monitor your eye health with exceptional accuracy. By catching problems early, we can protect your vision and preserve your quality of life.

Frequently Asked Questions

A: An optometrist is a healthcare professional specializing in the examination, diagnosis, and treatment of visual and eye health issues. They hold a Doctor of Optometry (OD) degree and are trained to perform comprehensive eye exams, prescribe corrective lenses, and detect various eye conditions such as glaucoma, cataracts, and macular degeneration.
A: The frequency of eye exams depends on your age, health status, and risk factors. Generally, the American Optometric Association recommends: Children: First exam at 6 months, another at age 3, and then every two years between ages 4 to 17. Adults (18-39): Every two years. Adults (40-64): Every two years or as advised by your optometrist. Adults 65 and older: Annually or as recommended. During an eye exam, an optometrist will assess your vision clarity, check for refractive errors (like nearsightedness or farsightedness), evaluate eye coordination and focusing abilities, and screen for eye diseases.
A: Contact lenses offer several advantages over traditional eyeglasses, including: Wider field of vision: Contacts conform to the curvature of your eye, providing a broader and more natural field of vision without frames obstructing your view. No fogging or splattering: Unlike glasses, contacts won't fog up in humid conditions or get splattered by rain. Better for physical activities: Contacts stay in place during sports and physical activities, offering greater stability and comfort. However, contact lenses may not be suitable for everyone. Individuals with certain eye conditions, such as severe dry eye, or those who are not diligent about lens hygiene, may face complications.
